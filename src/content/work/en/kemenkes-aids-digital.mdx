## Problem

As an activist on HIV/AIDS issues and founder of several health NGOs (including the Indonesian AIDS Coalition), Bani saw firsthand how HIV/AIDS case data collection and reporting in Indonesia still relied on slow, manual mechanisms that were hard to access across institutions. The Ministry of Health needed a digital system to support this issue in a more structured way.

## Solution

Bani built the **Digital AIDS Application** — a web-based system to support the Ministry of Health of Indonesia in digitally collecting and managing HIV/AIDS data. The project drew on Bani's own hands-on experience in health advocacy, bridging the government's technical needs with a sensitive social issue.

## Tools

- Web application (digital data collection system)
- Health database management
- Integration with government agency workflows

## Outcome

- **Officially adopted by the Ministry of Health of the Republic of Indonesia** in 2014, under then-Minister of Health Nafsiah Mboi.
- Bani received the **national MDG's Award** (Millennium Development Goals Award) for this project's contribution.
- This project stands as one of the proof points in Bani's track record of building digital systems for the government and public health sectors — part of his 18 years / since 2008 journey in the digital world.
