## Problem

The GWL-INA network (Gay, Waria, and Men who have Sex with Men in Indonesia) is a national advocacy network formed on February 4, 2007 in Surabaya, with chapters across various cities in Indonesia. As a nationwide network organization, its decision-making and leadership election process needed a mechanism that could fairly and transparently reach members across cities — something hard to achieve through in-person meetings alone.

## Solution

Bani built a **digital voting system** for the GWL-INA network, enabling members across cities to participate digitally in the organization's decision-making process. This project ran alongside the development of GWL-INA's official website, serving as the organization's information and communication channel to the public and fellow network members.

## Tools

- Digital voting platform
- Official network organization website
- Membership database

## Outcome

- The digital voting system was used by the GWL-INA network to support nationwide organizational decision-making, reaching member chapters across cities without requiring full in-person meetings.
- This project is part of Bani's track record building digital systems for the NGO and health advocacy sector — one of 5 NGOs he founded or supported on HIV, gender, and human rights issues.
